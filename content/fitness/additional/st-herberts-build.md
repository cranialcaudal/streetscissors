### The Goal & The Lap Key
*A round-trip open-water swim out to St Herbert's Island, Derwentwater and back — your target is **2km each way, ~4km total**. A focused 4-week peak: two hard pool sessions a week at the UC Davis ARC (these are no longer easy recovery days — the deliberate trade for 4 weeks).*

**Lap key — ARC pool, 1 lap = down-and-back = 50 yd ≈ 46 m:**
- 20 laps ≈ 0.9km · 44 laps ≈ 2km (**one leg / the island**) · 66 laps ≈ 3km
- **88 laps ≈ 4km = your full out-and-back.** Anchor: *44 laps gets you to the island, 88 brings you home.*

> **Route reality check:** the *direct* Calfclose Bay ↔ St Herbert's line is only ~1km each way (~2km round trip). A true 2km-each-way / 4km swim means a **longer route or extra crossings** (loop the islands, or double the out-and-back). Map your actual on-water line before race day so the distance matches this plan.

> ### ⚠️ The cold, not the distance, is what can hurt you
> 4km in skins is **~85–100 minutes of continuous immersion in ~16°C water.** That is roughly double the exposure of a 2km swim, and 90+ minutes in 16°C *skins* is cold-injury territory for anyone not adapted over **months**. Daily showers + 2 lake dips **cannot** build that — they blunt the acute cold-shock gasp, not long-duration cold tolerance. **For a swim this long the honest call is a wetsuit, or extending your cold timeline well past these 4 weeks.** If you hold to skins, Lake Trip 2 is a hard go/no-go: can't stay functional and warm for ~40 min there → wetsuit or shorten the swim. No ego.

### Time Goal & Expected Time in Water
*Pace targets are a steady, repeatable open-water effort. Calibrate off your Week 3 benchmark, which is your real 4km time trial.*

**Race-day time goal — 4km / 88 laps:**
- **Target: ~85–95 min**, steady at ~1:00–1:05 per lap (≈2:10/100m).
- **Strong day:** ~80 min (~0:55 per lap / ≈2:00/100m).
- **Honest range in skins + cold + chop:** 90–110 min — and *that* number is the whole problem. Plan the cold around ~95 min in the water, not 45.

**Pace cues by effort (per lap = 50 yd):**
- Easy / aerobic — ~1:05–1:10 (conversational)
- Steady / race pace — ~1:00–1:05 (strong, even, repeatable)
- Threshold — ~0:55 (comfortably hard)
- Speed (single lengths) — near-max

**Your real benchmark:** the Week 3 **island-simulation swim (2 × 44 laps) is your 4km time trial — put it on the clock**, turnaround included. That time, plus a few minutes for open-water sighting and chop, is your honest race-day expectation. The pool says whether your *engine* is ready; only the lake says whether your *cold tolerance* is.

**Expected time in water per session:**
- **Week 1:** Mon ~65 min · Fri ~60 min
- **Week 2:** Mon ~85 min · Fri ~65 min
- **Week 3:** Mon ~95–105 min (incl. the ~90-min benchmark) · Fri ~70 min
- **Week 4:** Mon ~35 min · Fri ~20 min
- **Lake trips:** Trip 1 = 10–15 min · Trip 2 = build to 40+ min (the go/no-go)

### The 4-Week Shape
- **Mondays = Distance.** Build the unbroken long swim toward the full 88-lap out-and-back. Open turns, **no push-off, no glide** — fake the no-wall lake.
- **Fridays = Race Simulation & Survival.** Sighting, bilateral breathing, sustained race-pace swims, deep-end treading/sculling (skins survival), a speed gear.

> Mondays land one day after Sunday's pitch. If the throwing shoulder is barking, drop the threshold reps and just swim the continuous block — protect the joint, keep the distance.

### Cold Plan — Showers Daily + 2 Lake Trips
*(Read the ⚠️ box above first — for 4km this plan is a floor, not enough on its own.)*
- [ ] **Daily cold shower, progressive:** Week 1 → 60–90s cold finish; build to **2–3 min, as cold as the tap goes** by Week 4. Breathe slow *through* the gasp, every day.
- [ ] **Cold face dunks:** basin of cold water + ice, submerge your **face** 10–15s, several reps. The face drives the gasp reflex hardest — the most lake-specific habituation you can get dry.
- [ ] **🧊 Lake Trip 1 — end of Week 2:** first immersion. Short: **10–15 min**, calm breathing through the shock, out while your hands work. Buddy + warm dry kit + hot drink on the bank *before* entry.
- [ ] **🧊 Lake Trip 2 — Week 4, ≥3 days before travel:** the go/no-go. Build to **40+ min** on a sighted line. If you can't stay functional and warm that long, **switch to a wetsuit** for the real swim.

> Use **genuinely cold** water (thermometer — aim 15–18°C). Lake Berryessa's summer surface is likely too warm; the cold dam-release reaches of **Putah Creek** below Monticello Dam run colder. Confirm temp + a safe, legal entry first.

### Week 1 — Build the Base
**Monday — Distance Base** *(~58 laps · ~65 min in water)*
- [ ] **Warm-up:** 8 laps easy, long and relaxed; exhale fully underwater
- [ ] **Drills:** 4 laps alternating catch-up / bilateral by length
- [ ] **Main — continuous:** **30 laps unbroken** (~1.4km), open turns, no push-off, steady aerobic
- [ ] **Threshold:** 4 × 4 laps comfortably hard, 30s rest
- [ ] **Cool-down:** 4 laps easy

**Friday — Skills & Speed** *(~48 laps · ~60 min in water)*
- [ ] **Warm-up:** 6 laps easy
- [ ] **Open-water rehearsal:** 3 × 8 laps continuous — **sight every 6 strokes**, breathe bilaterally for one full rep, 30s rest
- [ ] **Deep-end survival:** 4 × 2-min tread (hands out on minute 2) + 3-min back sculling
- [ ] **Speed:** 8 × 1 length (25 yd) fast, full rest — clean stroke, no thrash
- [ ] **Cool-down:** 4 laps backstroke

---

### Week 2 — Extend
**Monday — Extend Distance** *(~70 laps · ~85 min in water)*
- [ ] **Warm-up:** 8 laps + 4 laps drill
- [ ] **Main — continuous:** **50 laps unbroken** (~2.3km — past one full leg), even pace, open turns
- [ ] **Threshold:** 3 × 4 laps comfortably hard, 25s rest
- [ ] **Cool-down:** 4 laps easy

**Friday — Race Pace & Survival** *(~55 laps · ~65 min in water)*
- [ ] **Warm-up:** 6 laps
- [ ] **Race-pace broken swim:** 3 × 10 laps at goal pace, sight every 6, 45s rest
- [ ] **Survival:** 5 × 2-min tread + vertical kicking 4 × 45s + panic-breathing drill (exhale sharp, roll to back, recover *without* the wall)
- [ ] **Speed:** 8 × 1 length descending (each faster), 20s rest
- [ ] **Cool-down:** 4 laps
- [ ] **🧊 Lake Trip 1** this weekend (see Cold Plan)

---

### Week 3 — Hit the Distance
**Monday — The 4km Benchmark (Island Simulation)** *(~96 laps · ~95–105 min in water)*
- [ ] **Warm-up:** 8 laps easy + 4 laps build
- [ ] **THE BIG ONE — 2 × 44 laps**, each leg = the ~2km out to the island and the ~2km home, with a **1–2 min "island turnaround"** between (sight, regroup, breathe — but the cold clock never stops). **88 laps / ~4km. Put the whole thing on the clock, turnaround included — this is your race.** Open turns, steady even pace, sight every 6 strokes.
- [ ] **Cool-down:** 4 laps very easy

**Friday — Race Simulation & Survival** *(~58 laps · ~70 min in water)*
- [ ] **Warm-up:** 6 laps
- [ ] **Sustained race effort:** 40 laps continuous at goal race pace (~1.8km), sight every 6 — practice holding pace deep into fatigue
- [ ] **Survival refresher:** 4 × 2-min tread + sculling
- [ ] **Cool-down:** 4 laps easy

---

### Week 4 — Sharpen, Taper & Rehearse
*Volume drops, sharpness stays. You don't gain fitness this week — you arrive fresh.*

**Monday — Sharpen** *(~32 laps · ~35 min in water)*
- [ ] **Warm-up:** 6 laps
- [ ] 20 laps continuous, easy-moderate and smooth
- [ ] 4 × 2 laps at race pace, full rest — wake the gear up, no fatigue
- [ ] **Cool-down:** 4 laps

**Friday (or last pool day before travel) — Feel-Good Primer** *(~16 laps · ~20 min in water)*
- [ ] 6 laps easy → 4 × 1 lap building to race pace → 6 laps easy. Short and sharp; leave the water fast and fresh, never tired.
- [ ] **🧊 Lake Trip 2** earlier this week (see Cold Plan) — the dress rehearsal and your hard go/no-go on skins.

---

### Race Day — The Swim Itself
- [ ] **Acclimatize on entry:** 1–2 min of controlled breathing in the shallows before committing.
- [ ] **Swim it like your Week 3 island simulation** — long, smooth, even, two legs with a turnaround. The distance is banked; the job is staying calm and warm enough across ~95 min.
- [ ] **Bright cap + tow float**, map the out-and-back line and exit before you start, watch Derwentwater's motor launches, kayak/paddleboard escort strongly advised for a swim this long.

> **Safety (cold + long):** never lake-swim solo; warm dry kit + hot drink on the bank *before* you enter; afterdrop is real and worse the longer you're in — you keep cooling after you exit. At ~90 min the failure mode is gradual cold incapacitation, not a dramatic gasp: if your stroke shortens, your hands claw, or your thinking fogs, **get out — that is the swim telling you the wetsuit was the right call.**
