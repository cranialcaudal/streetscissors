**Where:** Davis (Bike or drive — alternate weekly to give your legs a break before Saturday)
**Focus:** Fast-twitch and in-water survivability. This is the *harder* of your two swim days.

If Monday is the engine, Friday is the gearbox and the airbags. Two hours, three jobs: hold open-water distance, survive without a wall under you, and find a genuine fast gear over short distances. Where Monday stays easy on purpose, Friday deliberately pushes intensity and skill.

The session is built to land on the upper body and core — shoulders, lats, treading legs, breath control — while sparing the cycling and running muscles you'll need for Saturday's brick. That's why the swimrun run segments stay short and smooth: they're transitions, not a workout. Get the speed and the survival work in, keep the legs fresh, and you arrive at Saturday ready.

> Five days clear of Sunday's pitch and two days ahead of the next one — this is the right window to ask the shoulder for speed. If anything sharp shows up, drop the fast block and keep the endurance and treading.
