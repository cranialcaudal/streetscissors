**Where:** Davis (Drive-in) — early in for the pool, 9:30 work start
**Focus:** Build the Ironman aerobic engine. This is the *easier* of your two swim days — all of it lives at easy, controlled effort.

Monday is your endurance day. Coming off Saturday's brick and Sunday's game, the body doesn't need more intensity — it needs volume at a pace it can absorb. Ninety minutes of smooth, aerobic freestyle builds the diesel engine that carries a 2.4-mile swim without spiking fatigue into the rest of the week.

Swimming before work now means an earlier alarm than a pure drive-in day would — that's the trade for getting the session done while it's quiet and uncontested. Keep every length easy; the whole point of Monday is that you could do it again tomorrow.

After work, pickleball is recreational — enjoy it, keep it social, and don't let it turn into intervals. Two court days a week (here and Thursday) is plenty of lateral, stop-start load on the knees and ankles.

> The throwing shoulder is one day removed from Sunday's pitch. Swim kindly — long and quiet, not thrashy — and lean on the kick and backstroke sets if it's still tender. Save the hard, fast, survival work for Friday.
