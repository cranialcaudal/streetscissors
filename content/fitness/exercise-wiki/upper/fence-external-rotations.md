**Goal:** Strengthen the posterior rotator cuff (infraspinatus, teres minor) using a band anchored to a fence — convenient pre-game arm care at the field.

### Execution
Anchor a band to a fence at about waist height. Stand side-on, pin the working elbow to your ribs at 90°, and rotate the forearm outward against the band, keeping the elbow glued in place. Don't let the torso twist or the shoulder shrug. Control the return.

### Why it works
EMG studies show that external-rotation exercises with the elbow at the side preferentially recruit the infraspinatus and teres minor while keeping deltoid activity low, so the load lands on the cuff itself (Reinold et al., 2004; Reinold et al., 2007). The posterior cuff is the primary brake on the arm during throwing follow-through, which is why this is a staple of a pitcher's warm-up — done at the fence, it primes exactly the tissue that protects the shoulder. See also [[band-external-rotations|band external rotations]].

### Programming notes
Light band, higher reps (2-3 x 12-20), elbow pinned and torso still, with a slow return. Activation and tissue health, not a max effort — ideal as part of a pre-throwing warm-up.
