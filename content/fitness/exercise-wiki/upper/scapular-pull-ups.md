**Goal:** Train scapular control at the bottom of a hang — pulling the shoulder blades down and back without bending the elbows. This is the foundation that makes full pull-ups safe and strong.

### Execution
Hang from a bar with straight arms. Without bending the elbows, pull your shoulder blades down and together so your whole body rises a couple of inches. Pause, then let the shoulders rise back toward your ears under control. All of the movement happens at the shoulder blades.

### Why it works
EMG work on scapular exercises identified which movements best recruit the lower trapezius and scapular stabilizers (Ekstrom et al., 2003). Owning the down-and-back scapular position — rather than hanging passively from the shoulder capsule — is what protects the shoulder under pulling load and sets the starting position for a strong [[pull-ups|pull-up]].

### Programming notes
2-3 x 6-10 slow reps, with a clear pause at the bottom (blades depressed). Strict and unhurried — this is control work, not a strength grind.
