**Goal:** Open and warm up the shoulders through a full overhead-to-behind range using a band or stick — a mobility primer, not a stretch to force.

### Execution
Hold a band (or dowel) with a wide grip, arms straight. Keeping the arms straight, raise the band overhead and continue back behind your body, then return the same way. Start with a grip wide enough that the range is comfortable, and narrow it gradually only as the shoulders open. Keep the ribs down rather than arching the back to find range.

### Why it works (theory)
The rationale is to take the shoulder through its full circumduction range under light, controlled tension, warming the joint and the surrounding tissue before overhead or throwing work. The wide grip keeps it within a safe range; the goal is gentle mobility, not stretching the capsule to its limit. Movement-prep principle rather than a tested protocol.

### Programming notes
1-2 x 8-10 slow passes, with a grip wide enough to stay comfortable. Never force the range or rush the tempo. A good warm-up before overhead or throwing.
