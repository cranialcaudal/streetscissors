**Goal:** Build vertical pulling strength through the lats and mid-back — the foundation of a strong, balanced upper body and a healthy throwing shoulder.

### Execution
Grip slightly wider than the shoulders. Starting from a controlled scapular set (see [[scapular-pull-ups|scapular pull-ups]]), pull your chest toward the bar by driving the elbows down and back. Squeeze at the top, then lower under control until the arms are straight. Keep the reps strict — no kipping or swinging — and use an assisted machine or band if needed.

### Why it works (theory)
The principle is twofold. First, the lats are major decelerators of the arm after a throw, so building pulling strength supports the shoulder's ability to brake. Second, the plan deliberately runs pull volume *ahead* of pressing volume: most shoulders accumulate too much pressing and too little pulling, and tilting the balance toward the back keeps the shoulder healthy. Pull-ups are the anchor of that pulling volume. (A general training principle rather than an exercise with a single defining study.)

### Programming notes
3 x 6-8 strict reps; assist as needed but keep form clean and stop short of failure if it degrades. Set the shoulder blades before each rep rather than yanking from a dead hang.
