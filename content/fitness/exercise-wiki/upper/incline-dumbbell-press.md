**Goal:** Bias the upper chest by pressing on an incline — rounding out chest development above what flat pressing trains.

### Execution
Set the bench to about a 30° incline. Press the dumbbells up and slightly together over the upper chest, elbows tucked modestly, and lower under control. Squeeze at the top. Avoid setting the incline too steep, which turns it into a shoulder press.

### Why it works (theory)
The principle is angle: changing the torso angle shifts pressing emphasis toward the clavicular (upper) fibers of the pec. It's primarily an aesthetic and balanced-development choice rather than a performance necessity. As with all pressing, keep it in proportion to pulling volume for shoulder health.

### Programming notes
3 x 8-10. A 30° bench is plenty; if the front delts take over, the incline is too steep. Bonus/aesthetic work — drop it first if time is short.
