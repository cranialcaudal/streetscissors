**Goal:** Strengthen the anterior cuff (subscapularis), which internally rotates the shoulder and contributes to the front-side stability that balances the heavily trained external rotators.

### Execution
With the band anchored to the fence, stand so the working arm is nearest the anchor. Elbow pinned to the ribs at 90°, rotate the forearm inward across the body against the band. Keep the elbow glued in place and the torso still; control the return.

### Why it works (theory)
The cuff works as a balanced unit, and the subscapularis is the main internal rotator on the front of that ring. The external rotators get the most prehab attention — and have the most direct EMG support (see [[fence-external-rotations|external rotations]]) — because they brake the throwing arm; internal-rotation work is included on the principle that a balanced cuff is a healthy cuff, not because the throwing motion lacks internal-rotation strength. Keep the emphasis tilted toward external rotation, and treat this as the balancing complement.

### Programming notes
Light band, 2-3 x 12-15, elbow pinned and controlled. Use less volume here than on external rotations to keep the cuff's front/back balance tilted appropriately for a thrower.
