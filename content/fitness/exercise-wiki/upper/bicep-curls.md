**Goal:** Directly train the biceps through elbow flexion — arm size and strength, plus some supporting role in arm deceleration and carrying.

### Execution
Keep the elbow pinned to your side. Curl the weight up by flexing the elbow, squeeze the biceps at the top, then control the lowering (eccentric) phase fully. Avoid swinging the torso to throw the weight up — if you need momentum, the load is too heavy.

### Why it works (theory)
This is straightforward isolation: loading elbow flexion builds the biceps. The principle worth noting for this plan is that the biceps also assist in decelerating the arm and contribute to grip and carrying strength, so a little direct work has carryover beyond aesthetics — but it remains accessory work, not a priority lift.

### Programming notes
2-3 x 10-12, strict, with a controlled eccentric. Dumbbells or cables both work. Accessory/aesthetic block.
