**Goal:** Build horizontal pressing strength through the chest, shoulders, and triceps, with dumbbells allowing a freer, more shoulder-friendly path than a barbell.

### Execution
Lie on the bench, feet planted firmly. Press the dumbbells up tracking over the chest, then lower under control until the elbows are at or just below chest level. Keep the elbows tucked to roughly 45° rather than flaring straight out to the sides, which crowds the shoulder.

### Why it works (theory)
Pressing is necessary upper-body strength, and dumbbells are chosen on a joint-friendliness principle: each arm moves on its own path, so the shoulder isn't pinned to a fixed bar position, and the range can run a touch deeper. For a thrower, the key is to *balance* this pressing with at least as much pulling (see [[pull-ups|pull-ups]] and [[dumbbell-rows|rows]]) so the shoulder doesn't drift into a press-dominant, rounded pattern.

### Programming notes
3 x 6-8. Elbows at ~45°, controlled lowering. Keep pressing volume at or below pulling volume across the week.
