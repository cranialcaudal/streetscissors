**Goal:** Strengthen the rear delts, rhomboids, and mid-traps that retract the shoulder blades — the muscles that balance all the pressing and pull the shoulders out of a rounded posture.

### Execution
Hold a band at shoulder height with straight arms, palms down. Pull it apart by driving the hands out and back, leading with the rear delts and squeezing the shoulder blades together. Keep the ribs down (don't arch), avoid shrugging, and control the return.

### Why it works
Surface-EMG analysis of scapular exercises mapped how this class of movement recruits the trapezius musculature that retracts and stabilizes the shoulder blade (Ekstrom et al., 2003). Building the muscles that pull the shoulders down and back balances the heavy pressing volume and counters the forward-rounded posture that crowds the shoulder — a foundation for a healthy throwing shoulder.

### Programming notes
Light band, strict form, high reps (2-3 x 15-25). A great warm-up or between-sets filler. Lead with the shoulder blades, not the hands.
