**Goal:** Build mid-back thickness and horizontal pulling strength, reinforcing the scapular retraction that balances pressing and supports posture.

### Execution
Staggered stance with one hand braced on a bench, back flat. Let the dumbbell hang, then pull it toward your hip — not straight up to the chest — squeezing the shoulder blade at the top. Lower under control to a full stretch. Keep the torso still; don't rotate to heave the weight up.

### Why it works (theory)
The rationale is balance and posture: horizontal pulling develops the mid-back and trains the shoulder blades to retract, offsetting the forward-rounding pull of pressing and daily slouching. A strong, well-positioned mid-back is the platform a healthy shoulder works from. Single-arm work also lets each side be trained equally and through a full range. Straightforward training principle.

### Programming notes
3 x 8-10 per arm. Pull to the hip with a flat back; squeeze the blade, control the lowering. Pairs naturally as the pull half of a press/pull superset.
