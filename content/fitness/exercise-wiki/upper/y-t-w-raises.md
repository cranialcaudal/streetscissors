**Goal:** Strengthen the small scapular stabilizers — lower trapezius, mid-trapezius, and rhomboids — through three complementary arm angles.

### Execution
Lie chest-down on an incline bench with very light dumbbells (or no weight to start), thumbs up.
- **Y:** Raise the arms overhead at about 45° to form a Y.
- **T:** Raise the arms straight out to the sides, squeezing the shoulder blades.
- **W:** Bend the elbows to 90° and pinch the shoulder blades to lift the forearms.
Move slowly and lead with the shoulder blades rather than the hands.

### Why it works
EMG analysis of trapezius exercises supports prone-raise variants like these for recruiting the lower and mid trapezius (Ekstrom et al., 2003). The three letters bias slightly different fibers of the scapular stabilizers, building the often-neglected muscles that hold the shoulder blade in place — the quiet base on which a durable, well-tracking shoulder depends.

### Programming notes
2-3 x 8 of each letter with very light weight (5 lb is plenty). This is control and endurance work; if you can swing the dumbbells up, they're too heavy. Slow and strict.
