**Goal:** Train the serratus anterior and lower trapezius to rotate the shoulder blade upward smoothly — the scapular motion that keeps overhead positions healthy.

### Execution
Stand with your back near a wall, forearms and the pinky-side of your hands against it, elbows below shoulder height. Slide the arms overhead while keeping contact with the wall, then return. Don't let the lower back arch or the ribs flare — keep a light core brace so the motion comes from the shoulder blades, not the spine.

### Why it works
Surface-EMG analysis identified exercises that effectively target the serratus anterior and lower trapezius (Ekstrom et al., 2003) — the muscles responsible for upwardly rotating the scapula as the arm goes overhead. When they do their job, the shoulder has room to move overhead without impingement; when they're weak, the shoulder hikes and crowds. Wall slides drill that pattern directly.

### Programming notes
2-3 x 8-12 slow reps, keeping contact with the wall and the ribs down. If you can't keep the lower back flat, slide through a smaller range. A good warm-up before overhead or pressing work.
