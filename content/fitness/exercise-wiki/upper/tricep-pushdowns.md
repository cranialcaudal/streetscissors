**Goal:** Isolate the triceps through elbow extension — supporting pressing lockout strength and arm development.

### Execution
Stand at a cable with a rope or bar, elbows pinned to your sides. Push the attachment down by extending the elbows, spreading the rope apart at the bottom to fully lock out the triceps. Control the return to about 90° without letting the elbows drift forward.

### Why it works (theory)
The rationale is targeted elbow extension: pinning the elbows removes the shoulder from the movement so the triceps do the work, building the muscle that finishes every press. It's accessory hypertrophy and lockout support rather than a primary performance lift.

### Programming notes
2-3 x 10-12, elbows pinned, full lockout. Pairs well supersetted with curls in a bonus arm block.
