**Goal:** Isolate the lateral (side) deltoid to build shoulder width — primarily an aesthetic and balanced-shoulder accessory.

### Execution
Use a light weight with a slight forward lean. Raise the dumbbells out to the sides, leading slightly with the pinkies, until the arms are about parallel to the floor. Don't shrug the traps up to assist, and lower under control rather than dropping.

### Why it works (theory)
The principle is isolation of a muscle that the big pressing and pulling lifts don't directly target much. Building the lateral delt rounds out the shoulder's appearance and contributes to balanced deltoid development. It is accessory work; keep the ego off the dumbbells, since heavy lateral raises just recruit the traps and stress the shoulder.

### Programming notes
2-3 x 12-15, light and strict, no shrugging. If you can't control the lowering, the weight is too heavy. Bonus/aesthetic block.
