**Goal:** On the surface, an easy jog raises core temperature and circulation before harder work. Underneath, it is a deposit into the **aerobic engine** — the low-intensity volume that builds the cardiovascular and metabolic base everything else is stacked on top of.

### Execution
Run at a genuinely conversational pace for 5–10 minutes (longer when it's the day's main aerobic work). The test is simple: you should be able to talk in full sentences, or breathe through your nose, without gasping. If your heart rate climbs or the talk test fails, slow down — even to a walk. The discipline here is *staying easy*; the most common mistake is drifting too hard and turning a recovery stimulus into a fatiguing one.

### Why it works
Foundational physiology showed that endurance-type exercise drives **mitochondrial biogenesis** — Holloszy's classic work demonstrated that training markedly increases the mitochondrial content and oxidative enzyme activity of skeletal muscle (Holloszy, 1967). More mitochondria means more capacity to burn fat for fuel, spare glycogen, and clear the byproducts of hard efforts — literally the machinery that lets you recover between intervals and hold a pace deep into a long event.

This is also why *easy* matters. Analyses of how the best endurance athletes actually train show that the large majority of their volume is accumulated at low intensity, with only a small fraction spent hard — a distribution associated with best-practice endurance development (Seiler, 2010). The easy jog isn't filler before the "real" training; for an aerobic athlete it *is* a large part of the real training, and keeping it easy is what makes the hard days productive rather than just tiring.

### Programming notes
Keep it strictly Zone 1–2 (conversational). Use it two ways: as a 5–10 minute warm-up to prime circulation before intensity, and as standalone easy volume to grow the aerobic base. The benefit comes from *accumulated* low-intensity time, so consistency beats any single session — and protecting the easy pace protects the quality of your hard sessions.
