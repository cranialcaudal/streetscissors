**Goal:** Prime the hip flexors, hamstrings, and fast turnover before any sprinting or fielding, so the first hard stride isn't taken cold.

### Execution
Over 15-20 yards, drive the knees up to hip height with a tall posture and quick ground contact. On the way back, snap the heels up to the glutes (butt kicks). Stay on the balls of the feet and keep the cadence high — these are quick, rhythmic drills, not slow marches.

### Why it works (theory)
The idea is to rehearse the two halves of the sprint cycle — the knee-drive (hip flexion) and the heel recovery (hamstring) — at submaximal speed. Doing so raises muscle temperature and runs through the neuromuscular pattern of sprinting before you ask for a maximal effort. Frame this as warm-up rationale rather than a training stimulus in its own right.

### Programming notes
Run them after the easy jog and before any sprint or agility work. 2 x 15-20 yards of each, crisp and controlled.
