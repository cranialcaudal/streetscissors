**Goal:** Build lateral hip mobility and quick feet — the grapevine pattern that shows up when you shuffle to field a ball or change direction on the bases.

### Execution
Move sideways down a line. The trailing leg alternately crosses in front of and then behind the lead leg while the hips rotate to allow it. Stay low, keep the feet fast, and let the rotation come from the hips rather than just stepping. Cover 15-20 yards, then return facing the same way so both directions get trained.

### Why it works (theory)
The rationale is movement variability. Straight-ahead running and barbell work train almost nothing in the frontal and rotational planes, yet that is where an infielder or outfielder actually lives. Carioca rehearses the cross-over hip rotation and rapid foot placement of changing direction, grooving the coordination at low intensity and warming the hips through a multi-directional pattern instead of a linear one. Treat the specifics as a coaching principle rather than a tested protocol.

### Programming notes
A warm-up drill. 2 x 15-20 yards each direction, building speed as the hips loosen. Keep it crisp; this is preparation, not conditioning.
