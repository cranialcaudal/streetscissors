**Goal:** Develop lower-body explosive power and rate of force development — the fast-twitch quality behind sprinting, fielding jumps, and a powerful first step.

### Execution
Load the hexbar lightly (roughly 10-20% of your deadlift). Dip into a quarter-squat and then jump straight up as explosively as possible, leaving the ground. Land softly with bent knees to absorb the impact, reset, and repeat. Every rep is about speed, not grind.

### Why it works
Plyometric and jump training reliably improves vertical jump height across studies, as shown in a meta-analysis of the method (Markovic, 2007). The hexbar is a practical tool for loading that jump: the same biomechanical work that found it produces greater force, velocity, and power than a straight bar also makes it well suited to expressing power under light load (Swinton et al., 2011). Together they train the legs to produce force *quickly*.

### Programming notes
3-5 sets of 3-5 reps with a light load, full rest between sets, and maximal intent on each jump. Quality over fatigue — stop the moment jumps slow down. Land soft; this is a power drill, not conditioning.
