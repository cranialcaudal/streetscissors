**Goal:** Build heavy, total-body pulling strength in a squat-hinge hybrid that loads the legs hard while sparing the spine — the cornerstone strength lift of the plan.

### Execution
Step inside the bar and grip the high handles. Set the hips, brace the trunk hard, and drive the floor away to stand tall, keeping the bar path vertical and the spine neutral. Lower under control by pushing the hips back. The high handles let you start more upright than a straight-bar pull.

### Why it works
A biomechanical comparison of the hexagonal (trap) bar versus the straight-bar deadlift found that the hexbar produced greater peak force, peak power, and peak velocity while *reducing* the moments — the loading stress — at the lumbar spine, hip, and ankle (Swinton et al., 2011). In plain terms: the centered load and high handles make the lift more upright and quad-involved, so you can build heavy strength with less shear on the lower back. That trade-off is exactly why it's the chosen heavy lift here.

### Programming notes
3 x 4-6 heavy reps. Brace before every rep and keep the spine neutral; the moment back position degrades, the set is over. Reset between reps rather than bouncing off the floor.
