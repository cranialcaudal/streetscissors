**Goal:** Load the posterior chain — hamstrings and glutes — through a hip hinge. This is the "pick a heavy object off the ground safely" pattern.

### Execution
Stand inside the bar holding the handles, knees softly bent. Hinge by pushing the hips straight back, lowering the bar along the legs while keeping a flat back, until you feel a strong stretch in the hamstrings. Drive the hips forward to stand tall. The knees stay relatively fixed — this is a hinge, not a squat.

### Why it works
The hexbar's centered load and high handles reduce the moments at the lumbar spine and hip relative to a straight bar while still allowing high force output (Swinton et al., 2011). That makes it a joint-friendlier way to load the hinge heavily — which is precisely the hamstring-and-glute strength and the real-world lifting capacity the plan is built around.

### Programming notes
3 x 5-8 reps. Hips back, not down; spine neutral; control the lowering phase to load the hamstrings through the stretch. Stop short of any rounding of the lower back.
