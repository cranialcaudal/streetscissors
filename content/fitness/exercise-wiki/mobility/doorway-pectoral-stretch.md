**Goal:** Open the chest and front-of-shoulder tissue that tightens from hunching over a bike, a desk, or a steering wheel.

### Execution
Stand in a doorway and place a forearm on the frame, elbow at about shoulder height. Step gently through the doorway until you feel a stretch across the chest and front of the shoulder. Vary the elbow height to bias different fibers of the pec. Keep the ribs down rather than arching the back to chase range.

### Why it works
Static stretching increases range of motion (Behm et al., 2016). A chronically tight chest pulls the shoulders forward into a rounded posture, which crowds the shoulder and works against the postural balance a throwing shoulder needs. Lengthening the pec helps the shoulder blades sit back where the scapular and cuff work can be effective.

### Programming notes
2-3 holds of 20-30 seconds per arm (and per elbow height), easy and controlled. Good as a posture-reset throughout the day.
