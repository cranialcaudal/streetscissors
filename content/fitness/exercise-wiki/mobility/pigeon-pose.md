**Goal:** Stretch the glutes and deep external rotators of the hip — tissues that tighten from running and sitting and that limit hip rotation.

### Execution
From all fours, bring one shin forward and across, lowering the hip toward the floor, with the back leg extended straight behind. Keep the hips square and ease the torso down over the front shin to deepen the stretch through the glute. Support yourself on the hands or forearms as needed.

### Why it works
Static stretching increases range of motion (Behm et al., 2016), and pigeon targets the glutes and external rotators directly. Freeing the hip's rotational range supports the stride and the rotation that throwing and hitting draw from the hips. As with other longer static holds, keep it after training or away from explosive efforts.

### Programming notes
2 holds of 30-60 seconds per side, easing in gradually. If the front knee complains, reduce the angle of the front shin or sit on a block.
