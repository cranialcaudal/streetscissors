**Goal:** Dynamically open the hips through swinging range — front-to-back and side-to-side — as part of a warm-up.

### Execution
Hold a fence or wall for balance. Swing one leg forward and back through a comfortable, progressively larger range (hamstring and hip flexor), then turn and swing it across your body and out (adductor and abductor). Keep the torso steady; let the leg swing freely. Do both planes on each leg.

### Why it works
Dynamic stretching prepares tissue and range without the brief power decrement that long static holds can produce, making it the preferred pre-activity choice (Behm et al., 2016). Swinging through an increasing range gradually takes the hips to end positions under control, readying them for the demands of sprinting, fielding, and the running stride.

### Programming notes
A warm-up drill. 10-15 swings per leg in each direction, starting small and building the range. Don't force the end range; let it open over the set.
