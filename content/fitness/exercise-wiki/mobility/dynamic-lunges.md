**Goal:** Open the hips and thoracic spine through movement — a dynamic warm-up that prepares the body to move rather than just holds a stretch.

### Execution
Step forward into a lunge, then rotate your torso over the front leg, reaching the arms across. Untwist, step the back foot up, and lunge forward on the other leg with a twist to that side. Keep moving down the line; the stretch is felt momentarily at the bottom of each rep, not held.

### Why it works
This is dynamic stretching, and a key practical finding of the stretching literature is that dynamic warm-up activity does *not* carry the transient power-loss penalty that prolonged static stretching can, while still preparing the tissues and improving usable range (Behm et al., 2016). The combined lunge-and-twist opens the hip flexor of the trailing leg and the thoracic spine in one movement — both of which feed the rotation a throw and swing require.

### Programming notes
A warm-up movement. 5-8 reps per side, flowing, moderate tempo. This is the kind of stretching to do *before* training, where static holds are not.
