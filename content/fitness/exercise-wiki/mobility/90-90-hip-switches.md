**Goal:** Actively open the hips into both internal and external rotation — the rotational range a throw, a swing, and a running stride all draw on.

### Execution
Sit on the floor with both knees bent to 90°: front shin across, back shin out to the side. Without using your hands, pivot on your heels and rotate both knees down and across to the other side, flowing smoothly between positions. Stay tall through the spine and let the movement come from the hips.

### Why it works (theory)
The rationale is that most hip stiffness is *rotational*, and it's the range people train least. Actively switching between the two 90/90 positions drives each hip through internal and external rotation under control, building usable, owned range rather than the passive range a held stretch produces. Treat this as a movement-quality principle; the specific drill isn't a tested protocol.

### Programming notes
Warm-up or daily mobility. 8-12 controlled switches, moving smoothly. Sit tall — if you collapse backward, prop the hands behind you until the hips open up.
