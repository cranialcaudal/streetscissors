**Goal:** Release the lats with a foam roller. The lats are major decelerators of the throwing arm, so keeping them supple matters for the shoulder.

### Execution
Lie on your side with the roller under your armpit and upper-back/lat area, top arm extended overhead. Roll slowly along the lat, pausing on tender spots and breathing. Rotate slightly to hit the muscle from different angles.

### Why it works
Self-myofascial release acutely increases range of motion without reducing muscle activation or force (MacDonald et al., 2013), and regular foam rolling improves flexibility with minimal performance cost (Pagaduan et al., 2022). Because the lats run from the arm to the pelvis and help brake the arm during throwing follow-through, supple lats support both overhead range and a healthy deceleration pattern — complementing the strengthening in pulls and rows.

### Programming notes
30-60 seconds per side, slow, exploring angles. Pairs well before overhead or pulling work, or in a cooldown.
