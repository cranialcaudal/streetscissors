**Goal:** Restore length to the hip flexors and quads — the muscles most shortened by running, cycling, and sitting.

### Execution
Place one shin vertically up against a wall or couch, knee on the floor, with the other foot planted in front in a half-kneel. Tuck the pelvis under (posterior tilt) and ease the torso upright until you feel a deep stretch through the front of the hip and thigh of the rear leg. Ease in gradually.

### Why it works
Static stretching reliably increases range of motion (Behm et al., 2016), and the couch stretch targets exactly the tissues an endurance-and-cycling athlete keeps in a shortened position all day. Lengthening the hip flexors lets the pelvis sit neutrally and the glutes work, instead of the low back compensating. As with all longer static stretches, place it after training or away from power efforts rather than immediately before them.

### Programming notes
2 holds of 30-60 seconds per side, breathing and easing deeper. Back off if it pulls at the knee; build the position gradually.
