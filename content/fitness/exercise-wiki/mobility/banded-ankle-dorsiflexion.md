**Goal:** Improve ankle dorsiflexion — the ability to drive the knee forward over the toes — which underpins squat depth, a clean running stride, and absorbing landings.

### Execution
Anchor a heavy band low and loop it around the front of the ankle, at the crease. Step the foot forward to create tension that pulls the ankle bone (talus) backward. Keeping the heel flat, drive the knee forward over and past the toes, then return. Work it straight ahead and slightly to each side.

### Why it works (theory)
The idea behind the band is a joint mobilization: the rearward pull on the talus is thought to give the joint room to glide so the knee can travel further forward without the heel lifting. Restoring dorsiflexion matters because a stiff ankle forces compensations up the chain (the knees and hips absorb what the ankle can't). This is a mobilization principle rather than a precisely validated protocol — judge it by whether your usable dorsiflexion improves.

### Programming notes
2 x 10-15 slow reps per ankle, heel staying down throughout. Useful before squatting or running.
