**Goal:** Lengthen the lats and obliques — big muscles that, when tight, limit overhead reach and rotation.

### Execution
Kneel and sit back into child's pose, arms extended forward. Walk both hands to one side and press the opposite hip down to feel a stretch run up the side of the torso from hip to armpit. Hold, breathe into the stretched side, then switch.

### Why it works
This is a static stretch, and static stretching is well established to increase range of motion (Behm et al., 2016). The lats attach from the upper arm down to the pelvis, so lengthening them frees up both overhead positions and rotation. The same review notes a practical caveat: prolonged static stretching immediately before explosive efforts can briefly blunt power, so save longer holds like this for after training or away from power work.

### Programming notes
2-3 holds of 20-30 seconds per side, relaxed breathing. Not the thing to do right before a max sprint or throw — use it as cooldown or standalone mobility.
