**Goal:** Restore extension to the thoracic spine — the upper-back motion lost to hours hunched over a bike or a desk.

### Execution
Place a foam roller across your mid-back, perpendicular to the spine. Support your head with your hands, and gently extend back over the roller, then return. Move the roller up or down a few inches to work different segments. Keep the ribs from flaring by keeping a light core brace; the motion is at the upper back, not the lower.

### Why it works
Using a roller on the thoracic spine is a form of self-myofascial release / mobilization, and acute SMR has been shown to increase range of motion without reducing muscle activation or force (MacDonald et al., 2013). Restoring thoracic extension lets the shoulders sit back and overhead positions improve — directly countering the rounded posture that cycling and desk work drive, and supporting healthy overhead and throwing mechanics.

### Programming notes
Work each segment for 20-30 seconds or 5-8 gentle extensions, then shift the roller. Keep the lower back out of it — brace lightly so the motion stays in the upper back.
