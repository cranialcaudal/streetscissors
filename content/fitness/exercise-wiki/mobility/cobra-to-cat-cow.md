**Goal:** Move the spine actively through flexion and extension to decompress it and counter the static postures the rest of the week imposes.

### Execution
Flow between two shapes. Cobra/upward extension: lying or on all fours, gently arch and open the chest. Cat-cow: on all fours, alternately round the spine toward the ceiling (cat) and let it sag while lifting the chest and tailbone (cow). Move segment by segment and breathe with the motion.

### Why it works (theory)
The rationale is simple movement of the spine through its natural ranges. After hours hinged over a bike or seated at a desk, taking the spine smoothly through controlled flexion and extension restores motion and circulation to the area. Frame it as a movement-and-decompression principle rather than a corrective with a specific measured effect.

### Programming notes
8-12 slow cycles, breathing with the movement. Daily is fine; especially useful after long rides or sitting.
