**Goal:** Improve rotation through the thoracic (upper/mid) spine — the segment that *should* do most of your rotating, so the lower back doesn't have to.

### Execution
Kneel on all fours and place one hand behind your head. Rotate that elbow down toward the opposite arm, then open it up toward the ceiling, following the elbow with your eyes. Keep the hips level and stationary so the movement comes from the upper back, not the lumbar spine.

### Why it works (theory)
The rationale is segmental specificity. Rotation is meant to happen mostly at the thoracic spine; when the t-spine is stiff, the body steals rotation from the lower back, which isn't built for it. Drilling rotation on all fours — with the hips pinned — isolates and rehearses motion at the right segment. Treat this as a movement-quality principle.

### Programming notes
8-10 rotations per side, slow, eyes following the hand. Keep the hips dead level — if they swing, you've left the thoracic spine. Good in a warm-up before pressing, throwing, or rotation work.
