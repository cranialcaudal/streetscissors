**Goal:** Take the shoulder through a controlled full-circumference range while the shoulder blade learns to move with the arm.

### Execution
Stand facing or side-on to a wall with one hand on it. Trace a large, slow circle with that hand against the wall — like sweeping around a clock face — reaching through every "hour," especially the overhead and low-behind positions. Keep the motion smooth and let the shoulder blade rotate naturally with the arm.

### Why it works (theory)
The rationale is to actively explore the shoulder's full range, including the overhead and reaching-back positions that everyday movement neglects, while encouraging the scapula to glide in coordination with the arm rather than the shoulder hiking up. It's an active-mobility and coordination drill — a principle for grooving healthy overhead motion rather than a tested protocol.

### Programming notes
3-5 slow circles per direction, per arm. Keep it smooth and within a comfortable range; spend extra time at the positions that feel stiff.
