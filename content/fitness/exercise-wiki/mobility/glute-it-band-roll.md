**Goal:** Use self-myofascial release on the glutes and the side of the leg to restore range and reduce the tightness that builds up in the hips from running and cycling.

### Execution
Sit on a foam roller and cross one ankle over the opposite knee (a figure-four) to bias the glute, rolling slowly and pausing on tender spots. Then roll the side of the leg from hip to just above the knee. Move slowly and breathe; this is exploration, not a race down the leg.

### Why it works
An acute bout of self-myofascial release has been shown to increase range of motion *without* a subsequent decrease in muscle activation or force — meaning you get the mobility benefit without trading away strength (MacDonald et al., 2013). A systematic review of regular foam rolling likewise reports flexibility gains with little performance downside (Pagaduan et al., 2022). For an athlete whose hips take a beating from repetitive flexion, that's a low-cost way to keep the glutes — the engine of the stride — supple.

### Programming notes
30-60 seconds per area, slow, pausing on tight spots. Good before training (to free up range) or after (to aid recovery). Keep it tolerable, not bruising.
