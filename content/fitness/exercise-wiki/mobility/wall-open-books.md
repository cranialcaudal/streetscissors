**Goal:** Open thoracic rotation — the rotational range a pitch and a swing demand of the upper back — with the help of a wall to keep the lower body still.

### Execution
Lie or kneel on your side next to a wall in a split stance, arms extended together in front. Keeping the hips and lower body fixed, sweep the top arm up and all the way across to the other side — opening like a book — following the hand with your eyes, then return. The hips stay put so the rotation is forced into the thoracic spine.

### Why it works (theory)
The principle is the same as the quadruped version: rotation should come from the thoracic spine, and pinning the hips ensures it does instead of leaking into the lower back. The "open book" takes the upper back through its rotational range under control, which feeds the separation between hips and shoulders that powers throwing and hitting. Treat as a movement-quality principle.

### Programming notes
6-10 controlled rotations per side, eyes following the hand, hips locked in place. Good in a warm-up before any rotational or overhead work.
