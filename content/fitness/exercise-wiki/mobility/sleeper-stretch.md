**Goal:** Restore internal rotation to the throwing shoulder by stretching the posterior capsule, which tends to tighten in people who throw.

### Execution
Lie on the side of the shoulder you're stretching, that arm out in front, elbow bent 90° so the forearm points up. With the top hand, gently press the forearm down toward the floor (into internal rotation) until you feel a stretch deep in the back of the shoulder. Keep it gentle and controlled — small movements.

### Why it works
Static stretching increases range of motion (Behm et al., 2016). Throwers commonly lose internal rotation as the posterior shoulder tissue tightens, and the sleeper stretch is a standard way to address that specific restriction. Because the shoulder is a vulnerable joint, this is done gently and within comfort rather than forced — the goal is to recover range, not to crank on the capsule.

### Programming notes
2-3 holds of 20-30 seconds, easy pressure, ideally with a brief 5-second gentle isometric push-back before easing further. Stop at a stretch, never a pinch — sharp pain means back off.
