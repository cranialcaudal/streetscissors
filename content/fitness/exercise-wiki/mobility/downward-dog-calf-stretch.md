**Goal:** Lengthen the calves and Achilles to support dorsiflexion and tolerate running volume.

### Execution
From a downward-dog position (hips high, hands and feet on the floor, body in an inverted V), pedal the feet slowly — press one heel toward the floor while the other bends, then switch. You can also hold both heels down for a steadier stretch.

### Why it works
Static stretching increases range of motion (Behm et al., 2016), and supple calves support the ankle dorsiflexion that a smooth running stride depends on. This pairs naturally with the loading work in [[single-leg-calf-raises|single-leg calf raises]]: load builds a resilient Achilles, mobility keeps the range. Keep longer static holds away from the moments just before fast or explosive efforts.

### Programming notes
Pedal for 30-60 seconds, then hold both heels down for 20-30 seconds. Useful in a cooldown or before easy running.
