**Goal:** Train the lateral trunk — obliques and quadratus lumborum — to hold a side plank, then add a controlled rotation by threading the top arm under and through.

### Execution
Set a solid side plank with the hips high and the body in one line. Reach the top arm up, then thread it under your torso and through the gap, allowing a small controlled rotation, before re-extending it to the ceiling. Keep the hips lifted the entire time.

### Why it works (theory)
Side-plank positions load the lateral trunk isometrically, and the reach-through adds a controlled rotary component on top of that base. The principle is lateral stability plus rotary control — the trunk's ability to brace against side-bending and twisting forces during one-sided athletic actions. Lateral-trunk activation is well documented in the broader literature; treat the exact reach variation here as a coaching principle.

### Programming notes
3 x 6-10 reaches per side. Hips stay high and steady — if they drop, drop to a knee-supported side plank instead.
