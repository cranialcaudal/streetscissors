**Goal:** Combine light conditioning with a dynamic anti-rotation challenge by driving the knees while the hands balance on an unstable dome.

### Execution
Take a plank with both hands on the dome, shoulders stacked over the hands, hips level. Drive the knees toward the chest one at a time at a controlled pace. The hips must not rock or rotate as the legs move — keep them square and level the whole time.

### Why it works
With the hands on the dome, every knee drive creates a small rotational and balance disturbance that the trunk stabilizers must cancel; instability surfaces are shown to increase that stabilizer demand (Behm et al., 2010). The honest trade-off is the same one that governs all unstable-surface work: you cannot produce as much force or speed as on the ground, so treat this as a stability-and-conditioning drill, not a power movement.

### Programming notes
3 x 20-40 seconds at a controlled pace. Prioritize square, quiet hips over speed; if the hips start twisting, slow down.
