**Goal:** Wake up the deep stabilizers and ankle/hip balance systems on an unstable surface — an activation and warm-up piece, not a strength lift.

### Execution
Stand on the flat side or the dome of a BOSU. Find a tall, balanced position, then slowly march, lifting one knee at a time while keeping the torso quiet and the standing leg controlled. Resist the urge to flail the arms; let the trunk and hip do the balancing.

### Why it works
Training on an unstable surface increases the activation of the trunk and stabilizing musculature and challenges balance — but it does so at the cost of force and power output, which is why instability work is best understood as a proprioceptive, activation, and joint-stability tool rather than a strength stimulus (Behm et al., 2010). Used as intended — to switch the stabilizers on — it is a sensible warm-up.

### Programming notes
Keep it slow and early in a session. 2 x 30-60 seconds. Don't chase difficulty; the value is control, not wobble.
