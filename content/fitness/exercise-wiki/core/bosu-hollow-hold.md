**Goal:** Hold a hollow-body position — a hard anterior-core isometric — with the added instability of the dome demanding constant micro-adjustment.

### Execution
Sit on the dome and lower back so your lumbar spine presses into it, then extend the arms overhead and the legs out to form a shallow "banana." Press the low back down throughout and keep the ribs from flaring. Breathe shallowly; do not hold your breath.

### Why it works
The hollow hold is an anti-extension isometric with high rectus abdominis demand, the same family of high-activation abdominal exercises captured in EMG comparisons (Escamilla et al., 2010). Placing it on the dome adds an instability component that further recruits the stabilizers as you fight to keep the shape (Behm et al., 2010). The combination trains the front of the core to stay locked while everything else is trying to move.

### Programming notes
3 x 20-40 seconds. The low back staying glued down is the whole exercise — shorten the levers (bend the knees, lower the arms) before you let the back arch.
