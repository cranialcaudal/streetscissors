**Goal:** Mobilize the shoulders through a loaded circle while the core resists the constantly shifting offset weight — a warm-up that pairs mobility with anti-movement stability.

### Execution
Hold a light kettlebell by the horns at chest height. Circle it slowly around your head, keeping it close, tracing a full halo. Throughout, keep the ribs down and the hips and torso still — only the arms and shoulders move. Reverse direction halfway through.

### Why it works (theory)
The idea is to take the shoulders through a controlled, loaded range while the trunk quietly resists the bell's changing line of pull, which would otherwise tip or rotate you. It is an activation and mobility drill — a principle-based pairing of overhead range and core bracing — not a tested strength protocol.

### Programming notes
Warm-up use. 2 x 5 circles per direction with a light bell. Keep it close to the head and slow; the core staying still is the quality marker.
