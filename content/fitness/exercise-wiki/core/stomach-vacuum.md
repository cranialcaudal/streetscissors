**Goal:** Build awareness and tone of the transverse abdominis (TVA) — the deep "corset" muscle that wraps the waist and supports the spine from the inside.

### Execution
Exhale fully, then draw your navel in toward your spine as if zipping a tight belt, and hold the drawn-in position. Keep breathing shallowly behind the brace — do not hold your breath. Can be done standing, seated, or on all fours.

### Why it works (theory)
The theory is that the drawing-in maneuver preferentially recruits the deep TVA somewhat independently of the larger surface abdominals, building awareness and tone of that stabilizer. An honest caveat: the "hollowing versus bracing" question is genuinely unsettled in the literature — full abdominal *bracing* is generally favored for stabilizing a heavily loaded spine, while the vacuum/hollowing drill is better understood as a deep-core awareness and waistline-tone exercise. Frame it as principle, not a load-bearing stability method.

### Programming notes
3 x 10-20 second holds with relaxed breathing. Best done fasted or on an empty stomach when learning the contraction.
