**Goal:** Build rotational control and oblique endurance — moving a load side to side through trunk rotation rather than flailing arms.

### Execution
Sit leaning back at a stable angle (feet down for easier, feet up for harder). Hold the bell at the chest and rotate it from one side to the other, driving the movement from the trunk and keeping the chest tall. Control both directions; don't just swing.

### Why it works (theory)
The rationale is rotational endurance and learning to control trunk rotation under load. An honest caveat belongs here: loading repeated rotation under a flexed, leaning-back spine is debated for long-term lumbar wear, so keep the load moderate, the tempo controlled, and treat this as accessory rotational endurance rather than heavy or ballistic work. Principle over prescription.

### Programming notes
2-3 x 10-16 total reps, controlled, moderate load. If the low back rounds or you start swinging, reduce the lean-back angle or the weight.
