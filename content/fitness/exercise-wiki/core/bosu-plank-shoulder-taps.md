**Goal:** Train anti-rotation — resisting the hips' urge to twist each time you unload one hand.

### Execution
Plank with hands on the dome (or feet on it for less challenge), feet a little wider than usual for a stable base. Tap one hand to the opposite shoulder, then return it, and alternate. The entire point is that the hips and torso do not rotate or rock as you lift each hand.

### Why it works
Lifting a hand removes a support point and creates a rotational torque toward the unsupported side; the obliques and deep abdominal wall must fire to keep the pelvis square. Anti-rotation plank variants are high-oblique-activation core exercises (Escamilla et al., 2010), and the unstable dome raises the overall stabilizer demand (Behm et al., 2010).

### Programming notes
3 x 8-12 taps per side, slow and deliberate. Widen the feet to make the hips easier to keep still; narrow them to progress. Quality is measured by how little the hips move.
