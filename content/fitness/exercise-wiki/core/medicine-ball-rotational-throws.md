**Goal:** Express rotational power — sequencing hips to trunk to arms explosively — the same chain that drives a throw and a swing.

### Execution
Stand side-on to a solid wall, holding the ball near the hip farthest from it. Load the back hip, then fire the sequence: hips rotate first, the trunk follows, and the arms finish by throwing the ball hard into the wall. Catch it on the bounce, reset completely, and repeat with full intent.

### Why it works
Rotational medicine-ball throw velocity has been shown to relate to baseball performance measures in collegiate players, which makes the throw both a trainable expression of rotational power and a rough proxy for that quality (Taniyama et al., 2021). Mechanically, it rehearses the proximal-to-distal sequence — power generated at the hips and transmitted out through the trunk — that a throw and a bat swing share.

### Programming notes
Speed is the point. 3 x 5-6 per side with a light-to-medium ball, maximal intent on every rep, and full rest between sets so quality never drops. Reset fully between reps rather than rushing.
