**Goal:** Walk under heavy load with a tall spine — the literal "carry a person" pattern that ties together trunk stability, posture, and grip strength simultaneously.

### Execution
Pick up the heaviest pair of dumbbells or kettlebells you can hold with a locked wrist. Stand tall — chest up, shoulders packed back and down, ribs stacked over the pelvis. Walk quickly but with controlled steps for the prescribed distance. The load hangs at your sides and will try to pull your traps upward and your torso into a lateral lean; resist both actively. The set ends when posture breaks, not when the legs tire.

### Why it works (theory)
With every step, the trunk must resist lateral flexion on the opposite side from the grounded foot. Bilateral loading doubles that demand simultaneously. The grip and forearm flexors are under continuous isometric tension throughout, making this one of the most time-efficient grip developers available. Because the implement hangs outside the body's center of mass (unlike a hex bar), the anti-shrug and anti-lean demands are somewhat higher — a useful training stress in its own right.

### Programming notes
3 × 40 yards, heavy. Go heavier over time; grip is the intended limiting factor. Use straps only if grip fails well before posture does — the grip work is part of the prescription.
