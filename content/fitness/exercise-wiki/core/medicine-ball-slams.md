**Goal:** Produce full-body explosive power in the vertical plane — reaching tall, then violently driving the ball to the floor with the lats and trunk.

### Execution
Hold the ball overhead with the body fully extended onto the balls of the feet. Then forcefully flex — crunching the trunk and pulling down with the lats — and slam the ball into the floor as hard as you can, following through into a slight hinge. Catch or pick up, reset to full extension, repeat.

### Why it works (theory)
The rationale is twofold: it trains a fast extension-into-flexion power expression, and it gives a safe outlet for genuinely maximal effort, since a slammed ball can't be dropped dangerously the way a barbell can be missed. It also trains the lats and trunk to *accelerate*, complementing the deceleration-focused arm-care work elsewhere in the plan. Slams aren't well isolated in the research literature, so treat the prescription as principle.

### Programming notes
3 x 6-8 with maximal intent, resetting to full extension each rep. Stop the set when the slams lose violence — this is a power drill, not a conditioning grind.
