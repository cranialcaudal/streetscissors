**Goal:** Build isometric grip endurance at an intensity that develops capacity without the tissue cost of maximal crimping.

### Execution
Grip a bar, crimp edge, or implement at roughly 50% of your maximal effort and hold for about 15 seconds. Rest and repeat. The load should feel firm but sustainable, not a near-failure clench.

### Why it works (theory)
The idea is submaximal isometric loading of the finger flexors: holding at a moderate intensity builds grip endurance and tendon tolerance for carrying and gripping tasks, while staying well clear of the high-strain ranges that aggravate finger tendons. Climbing and grip-training protocols differ in their specifics, so treat the dose as a principle to adjust to your own tolerance.

### Programming notes
3-5 x 10-15 seconds at ~50% effort, with full rest between holds. Back off immediately if you feel sharp finger-joint or tendon discomfort.
