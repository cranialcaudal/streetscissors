**Goal:** Flush the forearm with blood and build finger-flexor endurance — a primer and pump, not a max-strength effort.

### Execution
Take a gripper, ball, or bar and perform rapid, light squeezes — a fast pulsing rhythm rather than slow maximal crimps. Keep the contractions short and the pace high for the duration of the set.

### Why it works (theory)
The rationale is local circulation and finger-flexor endurance: light, rapid contractions drive blood into the forearm and train the flexors to keep working, without the joint and tendon stress of maximal crimping. It's a warm-up and capacity tool. Grip protocols vary widely and aren't tightly standardized in the literature, so treat this as principle.

### Programming notes
2-3 x 20-30 fast, light reps. Keep it submaximal — the goal is a pump and endurance, not a grind.
