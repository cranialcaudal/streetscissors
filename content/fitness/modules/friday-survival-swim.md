### Lunchtime Survival & Speed Swim (2 Hours)
*The week's hardest water session. Three jobs, in order: prove you can swim open-water distance, prove you can survive when there's no wall, and prove you have a fast gear. Monday built the engine — today builds the parts of you that keep you alive and let you surge.*

**Lap key (ARC pool):** 1 lap = down-and-back = 50 yd. For the short speed work in Block 3, count single **lengths** — one length = one wall = 25 yd = half a lap.

> **Why hard the day before Saturday's brick?** Because today's load lands on shoulders, lats, and core — Saturday is legs (bike + run). Keep the run portions in Block 3 short and smooth, not sprints, and your legs arrive fresh tomorrow. If your legs feel the runs at all, you ran them too hard.

---

### Block 1 — Open-Water Endurance (40 minutes)
*Continuous, race-realistic. Sustained but not redlined — this is "could I do this in a wetsuit in a lake" pace.*

- [ ] **Warm-up: 8 laps easy freestyle**, building the last 2 laps.
- [ ] **3 × 12 laps Freestyle**, 30s rest:
    - **Sight every 6 strokes** — lift the eyes just clear of the surface, snatch a look, drop back down. No head-craning.
    - **Breathe bilaterally** through at least one full 12-lap rep so chop on either side never rattles you.
    - **Pace it like a swim leg**, not a sprint — strong, even, repeatable.

---

### Block 2 — Treading & Survival (35 minutes)
*Deep end, no wall, no bottom. This is the block that matters when the day goes wrong — a cramp, a panic, a kayak you can't reach. Build the calm.*

- [ ] **5 × 3-Minute Tread Blocks** (15 min):
    - Minute 1: Standard tread, hands relaxed in the water.
    - Minute 2: **Hands out of the water** (collarbones exposed) — forces the legs and core to carry you.
    - Minute 3: Active float recovery on the back, slow breathing.
- [ ] **Sculling Ladder** (8 min): Float on your back, arms at your sides, propel yourself **head-first then feet-first** using only figure-8 wrist sculls. The most efficient way to move with zero arm fatigue — pure survival economy.
- [ ] **Vertical Kicking** (6 min): Upright in the deep end, arms crossed on chest, kick (flutter then breaststroke) hard enough to keep your chin clear. 4 × 60s, 30s float between.
- [ ] **Panic Protocol Drill** (6 min): Simulate swallowing water — exhale sharply, roll to your back, float, recover your breathing **without reaching for the wall**. Rehearse the calm so it's automatic when it's real.

---

### Block 3 — Swimrun Speed (40 minutes)
*Fast-twitch. Short, hard swim efforts with quick transitions — the gear that gets you out of trouble and onto feet. The swim is where you spend the fire; the run is just a smooth jog to the next entry. Count single **lengths** here — these efforts are too short for a full down-and-back lap.*

- [ ] **Prime: 4 × 1 length (25 yd) build** to fast, full rest. Wake up the fast-twitch fibers.
- [ ] **Swimrun Intervals — 6 rounds** (~28 min):
    - **3 lengths (75 yd) FAST freestyle** — near-max, controlled turnover, full stroke (don't shorten and thrash).
    - **Out of the water, ~200 yd easy run** (pool deck loop or treadmill) — *smooth jog, not a sprint*. This is the transition, not the workout.
    - **Back in, 20–30s float**, then go again.
- [ ] **Finisher: 4 × 1 lap descending** (1–4 getting faster), 20s rest. End on your fastest, cleanest lap.

> Keep the runs flat and easy — short brisk transitions only. If you want true hard run intervals, they belong on a day that isn't stacked in front of Saturday's brick.

#### Cool-Down (5 minutes)
- [ ] **4 laps easy backstroke + breaststroke**: Open the chest, externally rotate the shoulders, drop the heart rate. Walk back to the desk recovered, not rattled.
