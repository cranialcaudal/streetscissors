### Shoulder Triage — do this first (~2 min)
*You play two games a week — Sunday plus one weekday league game that lands on a different day each week. So the cuff can be beat up on any given Tuesday, or any morning that follows a game. A fast press or throw on a shoulder that hasn't recovered is how a rough week becomes a six-week layoff. Spend two minutes finding out which day you're having, then run the matching column. This gate decides the session — don't skip it, and run the same three checks on any training morning that follows a game.*

- [ ] **Run three quick checks:**
    - **Painful arc:** Slowly raise a straight arm out to the side and overhead. Note any pinch between shoulder height and ear height.
    - **External-rotation strength:** Elbow pinned to your ribs, bent 90°. Push the back of that hand outward against your other hand. Compare sides for pain or obvious weakness.
    - **Sleep test:** Did lying on that shoulder wake you up last night?

> **🟢 GREEN — feels normal: no pinch, no weakness, slept fine.**
> Run the session exactly as written below.
>
> **🟡 YELLOW — achy or tight, reduced range, but no sharp pain.**
> - **Skip the medicine-ball rotational throws** — the catch decelerates the cuff under load, not today.
> - Pressing: cut the dumbbell bench to **2 × 8 light**, or swap it for a **neutral-grip floor press** or **landmine press** (both keep the arm out of the impingement zone).
> - **Keep everything that pulls** — rows, pull-ups, Y-T-W, band external rotations — and add a third set of [[band-external-rotations|band external rotations]]. Pulling and cuff work flush the joint and help it recover.
>
> **🔴 RED — sharp pain, a painful arc you can't push through, real weakness, or it woke you up.**
> - **No pressing, no throws.** Today is rehab, not a lift.
> - Run the [[band-pull-aparts|band pull-aparts]] plus [[fence-external-rotations|fence external]] / [[fence-internal-rotations|internal rotations]] from your pitcher's warm-up, add light [[dumbbell-rows|rows]] and [[y-t-w-raises|Y-T-W raises]], and take your volume from grip and lower-risk pulling.
> - If RED keeps showing up, the cause is upstream — add up the real throwing load (Sunday's game, the weekday league game, any Thursday baseball practice, plus Monday's swim volume). A weekday game landing right before this session is the usual culprit. If it persists, get the shoulder looked at.

### Warm-up — shoulder prep (~7 min)
*You're warm from the ride, but your shoulders aren't.*

- [ ] **[[band-pull-aparts|Band pull-aparts]]** — 2 × 20 (light band)
    - How-to: Hold the band at shoulder height, arms straight, palms down. Pull it apart by squeezing your shoulder blades together — lead with the rear delts, not the hands. Keep ribs down, don't shrug, control the return.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WqdNDTTe-9g
- [ ] **[[band-external-rotations|Band external rotations]]** — 2 × 15 / side (lighter band)
    - How-to: Elbow pinned to your side, bent 90°. Rotate the forearm outward against the band — movement comes only from the shoulder, elbow stays glued. Don't let the torso twist. Light band, controlled.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KN1hpZaxUKY *(shown on a cable — identical movement with a band)*
- [ ] **[[wall-slides|Wall slides]]** — 2 × 10 (bodyweight)
    - How-to: Forearms and the pinky side of your hands against the wall, elbows below shoulders. Slide the arms overhead while keeping contact. Don't let your lower back arch or ribs flare — move the shoulder blades.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YIvNRUJp7_E
- [ ] **[[scapular-pull-ups|Scapular pull-ups]]** — 2 × 8 (bodyweight)
    - How-to: Hang with straight arms. Without bending the elbows, pull your shoulder blades down and back so your body rises a couple of inches. Pause, then let the shoulders rise back up. All movement is at the shoulder blades.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-ZIpSoTRsuE
### Power — rotational (~9 min)
*The throwing engine. You must move fast here. Take the full rest.*

- [ ] **[[medicine-ball-rotational-throws|Medicine ball rotational throws]]** — 3 × 6 / side (light-medium weight, 8–12 lbs)
    - How-to: Stand a few feet from a solid wall, side-on (so your shoulder points at it). Hold the ball near the hip furthest from the wall. Pivot off the back foot, twist your hips hard toward the wall, and throw the ball with everything you have. Catch it on the bounce, reset fully, throw again.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o9BC7lgN1bo
    - Rest: 60 sec
### Strength — antagonist superset (~22 min)
*Throwers need strong lats and mid-back to decelerate the arm. Pressing is necessary, but the pull leads.*

**Superset:** Perform A1, rest briefly, perform A2, rest fully, then repeat.

- [ ] **A1. [[dumbbell-bench-press|Dumbbell bench press]]** — 3 × 6–8
    - Why: Necessary horizontal push. Dumbbells allow the shoulder to move freely without being pinned like a barbell.
    - How-to: Lie on the bench, feet planted hard. Press the dumbbells up, tracking over the chest. Lower with control until the elbows are at or just slightly below the chest. Don't let the elbows flare straight out to the sides — tuck them to about 45°.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YQ2s_Y7g5Qk
    - Rest: 45 sec
- [ ] **A2. [[pull-ups|Pull-ups]]** — 3 × 6–8 (use an assisted machine or band if necessary, but keep the reps strict)
    - Why: The vertical pull. Builds lat strength for throwing deceleration.
    - How-to: Grip slightly wider than shoulders. Pull your chest toward the bar by driving your elbows down and back. Squeeze at the top. Lower with control until arms are straight. Stop short of failure if form breaks down (kipping/swinging).
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MhokcbRLP5w
    - Rest: 90 sec
### Pull + shoulder health (~15 min)

**Superset:** Perform B1, rest briefly, perform B2, rest fully, then repeat.

- [ ] **B1. [[dumbbell-rows|Dumbbell rows]]** — 3 × 8–10 / arm
    - Why: Horizontal pull for mid-back thickness and shoulder retraction.
    - How-to: Staggered stance, one hand braced on a bench. Keep the back flat. Start with the dumbbell hanging, pull it toward your hip (not straight up to your chest). Squeeze the shoulder blade at the top. Lower with control.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pYcpY20QaE8
    - Rest: 30 sec
- [ ] **B2. [[y-t-w-raises|Y-T-W raises]]** — 3 × 8 of each letter (very light dumbbells, e.g., 5 lbs)
    - Why: Small stabilizer muscles (lower traps, rhomboids, rear delts).
    - How-to: Lie chest-down on an incline bench.
        - **Y:** Arms at 45° angle, thumbs up. Raise arms straight up.
        - **T:** Arms straight out to the sides, thumbs up. Raise arms up, squeezing shoulder blades.
        - **W:** Bend elbows to 90°, thumbs up. Pinch shoulder blades together to lift the forearms parallel to the floor.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QdGTI4Lshg4
    - Rest: 60 sec
### Grip finisher (~6 min)
*The throwing hand requires forearm and finger strength.*

- [ ] **[[farmers-walk|Farmer's Walks]]** — 3 × 40 yards (heavy dumbbells or kettlebells)
    - How-to: Pick up the heaviest pair you can hold with a locked wrist and a tall spine. Shoulders packed back and down — do not let them creep up. Walk quickly but with control for 40 yards. Go heavier over time; grip is the limiting factor and that is the point.
    - Rest: 60 sec

## Block 2 — Bonus aesthetic (~20 min)

If you're short on time, leave now. If you want to look good, stay.

### Chest (~14 min)

- [ ] **[[incline-dumbbell-press|Incline dumbbell press]]** — 3 × 8–10
    - Why: Upper chest.
    - How-to: Set bench to a 30° incline. Press similar to the flat bench, elbows tucked slightly. Squeeze at the top.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0G2_XV7slIg
    - Rest: 60 sec
- [ ] **[[cable-crossovers|Cable crossovers]]** — 3 × 12–15
    - Why: Chest isolation and pump.
    - How-to: Set pulleys high. Step forward, slight forward lean. Keep a slight bend in the elbows, bring your hands together in front of your chest (like hugging a barrel). Focus on the chest squeeze.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=taI4XduLpTk
    - Rest: 60 sec
### Arms & delts (~7 min, superset)

**Superset:** Perform C1, rest briefly, perform C2, perform C3, rest fully, then repeat.

- [ ] **C1. [[bicep-curls|Bicep curls]]** — 2 × 10–12 (dumbbells or cables)
    - How-to: Keep the elbow pinned to your side. Curl the weight up, squeeze the bicep. Control the eccentric (lowering) phase.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sAq_ocpRh_I
    - Rest: 0 sec (move to C2)
- [ ] **C2. [[tricep-pushdowns|Tricep pushdowns]]** — 2 × 10–12 (cable rope)
    - How-to: Elbows pinned to your sides. Push the rope down, spreading it apart at the bottom to lock out the triceps.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2-LAMcpzODU
    - Rest: 0 sec (move to C3)
- [ ] **C3. [[lateral-raises|Lateral raises]]** — 2 × 12–15 (dumbbells)
    - How-to: Light weight. Slight lean forward. Raise the dumbbells to the sides, leading slightly with the pinkies, until arms are parallel to the floor. Don't shrug.
    - Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3VcKaXpzqRo
    - Rest: 60 sec
