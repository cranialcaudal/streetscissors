### Phase 1: Neural Prep & Power
*Goal: Safe, explosive intent before loading the spine.*

- [ ] **Warm-up & Mobility**: [[90-90-hip-switches|90/90 hip switches]], dynamic lunges, unweighted squats.
- [ ] **[[hexbar-jumps|Hexbar Jumps]] or Box Jumps**: 3 x 3-5 reps. If using the hexbar, use very light weight (10-20% of your max), drop your hips, and explosively jump straight up off the floor. Land soft.
### Phase 2: Heavy Hexbar Power & Hinge
*Goal: Raw neural strength to build durability without adding dead mass for the Ironman.*

- [ ] **Main Power (Squat Pattern)**: [[hexbar-deadlift|Hexbar Deadlift using High Handles]] (3 x 4-6 reps). Using the high handles forces a more upright, quad-dominant position similar to a squat, but with zero spinal loading.
- [ ] **Main Hinge (Lift-a-Person)**: [[hexbar-rdl|Hexbar Romanian Deadlifts (RDLs)]] (3 x 5-8 reps). The hexbar is the absolute best tool for simulating picking a heavy object off the ground.
### Phase 3: The "Carry a Person" Pattern & Durability
*Goal: Building the chassis to walk with 150 lbs in your arms, and bulletproofing the lower leg.*

- [ ] **Carry-a-Person Core**: [[hexbar-carries|Heavy Hexbar Carries]] (3 sets of 40 yards). Step inside the hexbar, pick it up, and walk with control. Maximizes core bracing.
- [ ] **Ironman Durability**: [[single-leg-calf-raises|Single-leg calf raises]] (3 x 15 reps). Bulletproofing the Achilles for the heavy run volume.
