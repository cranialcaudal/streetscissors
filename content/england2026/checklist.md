# 🏴 England Trip — Packing Checklist

*Three containers: your **Deuter backpack** (carry-on + daypack), what rides **on the bike**, and the **SCICON case** (checked — holds the bike plus most of your clothes as padding).*

---

## 🎒 Deuter Race EXP Air 14+3 — carry-on & daypack

Valuables, electronics, and one day of essentials in case the bike case is delayed. It's a small bag (~17L), so this is carry-on priorities only — the rest goes in the bike case. Doubles as your conference/city/hiking daypack and the bag you stash in the locker.

- [ ] Passport + wallet + cards
- [ ] UK ETA confirmation, travel insurance details, train & accommodation confirmations
- [ ] Laptop + charger
- [ ] Power bank / portable charger ⚠️ *lithium batteries are carry-on only — never checked*
- [ ] Phone + charger + headphones
- [ ] UK (Type G) plug adapter
- [ ] Any medications
- [ ] Sunglasses
- [ ] One change of clothes (tee, underwear, socks) + toothbrush & mini toiletries (<100 ml)
- [ ] Earplugs + eye mask (plane *and* the YHA dorm)
- [ ] Kudu Docs — wear on the plane, then drop straight at the cobbler

---

## 🚲 On the bike — front rack/harness · frame bag · seat post bag · bottles

Ride-day essentials only; keep weight off your back, especially for the Fred.

> **Heads-up:** most Giant TCRs have no rack mounts (race fork/frame), so a strap-on **handlebar harness/roll** may fit better than a bolt-on front rack — same job for the rolled layers.

- [ ] Front rack **or** handlebar roll → rolled **rain jacket** (+ hoodie / jean jacket on the Penrith→Buttermere transfer day)
- [ ] 2 water bottles
- [ ] Helmet *(worn)*
- [ ] Cycling gloves + sunglasses *(worn)*
- [ ] **Frame bag:** 2 spare tubes · multitool · tire levers · CO2 + inflator · quick link · patches · chain lube · snacks
- [ ] **Seat post bag:** spare tube · mini-pump · small rag · packable layer
- [ ] Front + rear lights
- [ ] Phone mount / GPS for navigation
- [ ] Light bike lock (café & conference stops)
- [ ] Chamois cream *(apply before long rides)*
- [ ] CamelBak Rim Runner X22 — optional on big/remote ride days; ideal for the Buttermere hikes

---

## 🧳 SCICON AeroComfort 3.0 — checked bike case (flights only)

The bike mounts inside and your soft goods pad it, so most clothing travels here. ⚠️ **Watch total weight** — airline sports-equipment allowance is usually ~23–32 kg. No valuables, laptop, or power bank in here.

**The bike**
- [ ] Bike (wheels off → side pockets · pedals off · bars/seatpost set per SCICON)
- [ ] Frame bag + seat post bag + front rack/harness (packed, not mounted)
- [ ] Cycling shoes (cleated)
- [ ] Helmet — nestled in clothing for protection (or carry it on)

**Clothing (doubles as bike padding)**
- [ ] 5 athletic shorts
- [ ] 7 t-shirts
- [ ] 10 underwear
- [ ] 10 socks
- [ ] 1 jeans
- [ ] 2 slacks
- [ ] 2 polos
- [ ] 2 nice shirts
- [ ] 2 MLB jerseys
- [ ] 2 swim trunks
- [ ] Jean jacket + hoodie
- [ ] Cycling bibs + cycling jersey
- [ ] Sandals + **trail running shoes** (for the swimrun)
- [ ] Quick-dry travel towel
- [ ] **Swim gear:** water buoy (tow float) · swimcap · goggles

**Tools, liquids & extras** ⚠️ *must be checked, not carry-on*
- [ ] Multitool / anything with a blade
- [ ] CO2 cartridges *(check your airline's limit — often a few allowed checked, none in the cabin)*
- [ ] Spare tubes · chain lube · full-size toiletries · sunscreen
- [ ] Padlock for the YHA locker
- [ ] Electrolyte tabs · blister care / first-aid
- [ ] Packing cubes / dry bags + a couple of ziplocks

---

## ✅ Before you fly

- [ ] Apply for the **UK ETA** (at least 3 working days ahead)
- [ ] Buy: bibs · jersey · rain jacket · rack/harness · plug adapter · tubes + CO2 · travel towel · padlock · **tow float · swimcap · goggles**
- [ ] One **fully loaded test ride**
- [ ] Confirm the cobbler's turnaround for the Docs
- [ ] Check airline **bike-bag fee + CO2 policy**
- [ ] Photograph the packed bike (for reassembly + insurance)

---

## 📦 Coming home

- [ ] Trinkets / comic books / records — leave a little room, or ship separately *(records are heavy and warp easily in a bike case — a dedicated mailer is safer)*
