T## The Philosophy: Finish Strong & Stay Capable
This plan reconciles three competing priorities into a sustainable 7-day schedule:
1. **Ironman Triathlon (3–6 months out):** The primary endurance goal. The priority is to *finish strong* rather than chasing elite time splits. This allows the endurance volume to remain largely aerobic (Zone 2), lowering the recovery cost and leaving room for heavy strength training.
2. **Functional Strength:** Building heavy leg strength (squats/hinges), throwing arm durability for baseball (rotational power, scapular control), and real-world carrying capacity (lifting a 150lb person via grip and loaded carries).
3. **Logistics & Recovery:** Geographically locking facility-dependent work (swimming, lifting) to Davis commute days, while strictly protecting two days for recovery to survive the training load.

---

## Geographic & Commute Rules
- **Davis Days (Mon, Tue, Thu):** Facility-locked training happens here.
- **Sacramento Days (Wed, Fri, WFH):** Only remote-friendly training (running, mobility, rest).
- **The Bike Commute:** The 45–60 minute ride from Sacramento to Davis on Tuesdays and Thursdays is not a "tax" — it is free Zone 2 cycling volume. Gym sessions on these days are bolted onto a ride you are already doing.

## The 7-Day Structure

### Monday — True Recovery
- **Where:** Davis (Drive-in)
- **Training:** Easy lunch swim.
- **Focus:** You are most depleted Sunday night after the weekend brick and baseball game. Monday is about restorative movement. No heavy legs, no court sports.

### Tuesday — Upper Body & Power (Commute Day)
- **Where:** Davis (Bike commute in/out)
- **Training:** Gym (Upper Body) + Zone 2 Cycling.
- **Focus:** Horizontal/vertical pressing, rows, pulldowns. Dedicated arm-care for baseball pitching (posterior cuff, scapular control). Explosive rotational power (med-ball throws) and grip/bicep work for carrying capacity.

### Wednesday — The Long Run (WFH)
- **Where:** Sacramento
- **Training:** Evening Long Run.
- **Focus:** Working from home means zero commute fatigue. Fresh off Monday's rest and Tuesday's upper-body day, this is the safest place for the true Ironman long run (75+ minutes and growing).

### Thursday — Lower Body Strength (Commute Day)
- **Where:** Davis (Bike commute in/out)
- **Training:** Gym (Lower Body) + Zone 2 Cycling + Recreational Pickleball (Evening).
- **Focus:** Dedicated leg strength without Olympic lifting complexity. Heavy squats, heavy hinges, loaded carries (the literal "carry a person" pattern), plus single-leg and calf work for run/bike durability.

### Friday — Active Rest (WFH)
- **Where:** Sacramento / Davis (every other week)
- **Training:** Easy, longer swim.
- **Focus:** Active recovery. Keeping the blood flowing and clearing fatigue so you arrive at Saturday fresh. No intense efforts.

### Saturday — The Ironman Brick
- **Where:** Flexible (Folsom simulation every other week)
- **Training:** 40–60 mile cycle + 3–10 mile run.
- **Focus:** Race-specific endurance. Training the skill of running on tired legs separately from the Wednesday long run.

### Sunday — Gameday
- **Where:** Baseball diamond
- **Training:** 60-Minute Pre-Game Pitcher's Warmup + Baseball Game.
- **Focus:** Competing and protecting the arm. The pre-game warmup is heavily modularized (cardio/footwork, tissue release, rotational separation with wall open books, scapular band activation, and grip prep) to safeguard the UCL and shoulder capsule before the first pitch.

---

## Core Rules for Success
1. **Protect Monday and Friday:** These are the load-bearing recovery days. Do not let recreational court sports or heavy lifting creep into them. You do not adapt during training; you adapt during rest.
2. **Strength Over Mass:** Hypertrophy requires a calorie surplus, which fights against Ironman volume. The goal right now is *neural strength* and durability. Mass-building is an off-season project.
3. **Trim the Fat:** As the race approaches (final 12 weeks), drop the recreational court sports and extra bag work to protect the joints and prioritize race-specific volume.
